interactive cell-lineage simulator

Press play on one cell. Watch a living lineage tree bloom.

A single seed cell divides on a stochastic clock, each daughter inheriting a mutated copy of its mother's genome. Run it forward and the exact structure biologists use to trace ancestry grows live on your screen — not a static textbook diagram, a toy you drive.

Real biology, three rules

  1. 01

    A seed cell divides on a stochastic clock

    Each cell's division timing is drawn from a mean interval with jitter — real cells don't fire on a metronome, and neither does this one.

  2. 02

    Daughters inherit a mutated genome

    On division, hue, size, and division bias drift by a tunable mutation rate. Zero mutation holds one colour; crank it and the tree fans into visible sub-lineages.

  3. 03

    The tree lays itself out live

    A radial dendrogram partitions each parent's angular span by subtree size, so hundreds of cells branch without ever overlapping — while the camera keeps the whole colony in frame.

Four knobs, one living system

Mutation rate

Single hue to riot of colour lineages.

Division interval

Slow study to a sprinting colony.

Timing jitter

Synchronised waves to staggered chaos.

Max population

Set the size of the saturation moment.

Every run is a pure function of its seed and these four params — the seed lives in the URL, so a striking run is one link away from being shared exactly.
